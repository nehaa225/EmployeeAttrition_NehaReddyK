# Employee Attrition Prediction using Machine Learning

This repository contains the deliverables for the **Employee Attrition Prediction** project (Internship Project — Week 2). It implements a Machine Learning pipeline to predict employee turnover based on factors like job satisfaction, salary, work-life balance, tenure, and role.

## 🎯 Key Findings & Insights
* **Best Model:** **Logistic Regression** (with balanced class weights) is the best predictor of employee exits, achieving an overall predictive effectiveness (**ROC-AUC**) of **0.8035** and a **Recall of 66.0%** (capturing nearly two-thirds of actual departures).
* **Top 3 Drivers of Attrition:**
  1. **Working Overtime:** The single strongest driver of exits. Overtime employees are **3.5x more likely to leave** due to burnout.
  2. **Frequent Business Travel:** Frequent travelers experience an attrition rate of **24.9%** (vs. 11.5% for non-travelers).
  3. **Low Salaries:** Employees who left had a median salary of **$3,202/month** (38% lower than the $5,204 median for stayed employees).
* **Highest-Risk Segments:**
  * **Sales Representatives** (39.8% attrition rate) and **Laboratory Technicians** (23.9% attrition rate).
  * **New Hires** in their first 12–24 months (exceeding 34% attrition rate).
  * Employees reporting a **Work-Life Balance score of 1 (Bad)** (31.3% attrition rate).
